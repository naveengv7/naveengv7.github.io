Smartphone-based Iris Recognition through High-Quality Visible Spectrum Iris Capture

Authors: Naveenkumar G Venkataswamy, Yu Liu, Surendra Singh, Soumyabrata Dey, Stephanie Schuckers, Masudul H Imtiaz

Conference: arXiv preprint arXiv:2412.13063

DOI: https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.2412.13063

Objective

This study explores the feasibility of iris recognition using smartphone cameras in the visible spectrum (VIS). Unlike traditional near-infrared (NIR) imaging, this approach leverages high-resolution smartphone cameras to capture high-quality iris images. The goal is to enhance smartphone-based biometric authentication while ensuring compliance with ISO/IEC 29794-6 biometric standards.

Methodology

  • Android Application: A dedicated app was developed for capturing high-quality VIS iris images using automated focus and zoom adjustments.
  • YOLO v3-Tiny for Eye Detection: Integrated for real-time and accurate eye and iris detection.
  • G-ATTU-Net for Iris Segmentation: A lightweight deep-learning model optimized for mobile devices.
  • Dataset: The Clarkson University Visible Iris (CUVIRIS) dataset was created, consisting of **VIS and NIR iris images** from 47 subjects.
  • Evaluation: Performance was assessed using True Acceptance Rate (TAR) and cross-spectral matching between VIS and NIR images.

Results

  • VIS images achieved a TAR of 96.57%, demonstrating strong iris recognition performance in the visible spectrum.
  • NIR images achieved a TAR of 98.95%, maintaining superior accuracy.
  • Cross-spectral matching (NIR to VIS) resulted in a TAR of 96.17%, showing promising interoperability.
  • System performance remained consistent across different iris colors and capture distances.

Conclusion

This study presents a robust and efficient smartphone-based iris recognition system that adheres to biometric quality standards. The proposed approach, integrating advanced deep learning models with real-time processing, demonstrates the potential of smartphone cameras for high-accuracy iris recognition. The publicly available CUVIRIS dataset will further contribute to advancements in the field. Future work will focus on improving segmentation models and testing under more challenging environments.
