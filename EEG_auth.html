Support Vector Machine for Person Classification Using the EEG Signals

Authors: Naveenkumar G Venkataswamy, Masudul H Imtiaz

Conference: 2023 International Conference on Electrical, Computer and Energy Technologies (ICECET), 2023

DOI: 10.1109/ICECET58911.2023.10389511

Objective

This study explores the use of Electroencephalogram (EEG) signals for biometric authentication by identifying individuals based on their unique brain activity patterns. Traditional authentication methods like passwords and fingerprint recognition are vulnerable to spoofing, while EEG-based biometrics offer a more secure and forgery-resistant alternative.

Methodology

  • Dataset: EEG data from 12 subjects recorded using an OpenBCI helmet with 8 channels.
  • Task: Subjects performed an oddball auditory task to generate distinct EEG responses.
  • Feature Extraction: Statistical and spectral features such as:
    • Root Mean Square (RMS)
    • Shannon entropy
    • Power Spectral Density (PSD)
    • Hjorth parameters
  • Dimensionality Reduction: Applied Principal Component Analysis (PCA) to retain 27 key features.
  • Classifier Used: Support Vector Machine (SVM) with different kernel functions (Linear, Polynomial, and Radial Basis Function (RBF)).

Results

  • The RBF SVM achieved the highest classification accuracy of 92.9%, outperforming other kernel functions.
  • Linear SVM: 81.1% accuracy.
  • Polynomial SVM: 84.7% accuracy.
  • The study confirms that EEG-based authentication is feasible and could serve as a robust security mechanism.

Conclusion

  • This research highlights the potential of EEG signals for user authentication, providing a highly secure and spoof-resistant biometric alternative.
  • While the study used an existing dataset, future work with purpose-collected data could further enhance classification accuracy and real-world applicability.
